# Consumer Shopping Behaviour Analysis - SQL Project

## Project Overview

This is a **pure SQL project** demonstrating end-to-end data exploration and insight generation using only MySQL to analyse consumer purchasing behaviour

**Objective**  
Analyze 3,900 unique customer purchase records to answer real-world retail questions about demographics, purchase behavior, discounts, subscriptions, and purchase frequency patterns.

**Dataset**  
- Original file: `shopping_behavior_updated (1).csv` (7,800 rows with exact duplicates)  
- After cleaning: 3,900 unique transactions  
- Columns: Customer_ID, Age, Gender, Item_Purchased, Category, Purchase_Amount_USD, Location, Size, Color, Season, Review_Rating, Subscription_Status, Discount_Applied, Previous_Purchases, Payment_Method, Frequency_of_Purchases

**Business Context**  
Simulated e-commerce / retail company wanting to understand:  
- Who are the highest-value customers?  
- Do discounts lift or compress order value?  
- Does the subscription program drive retention?  
- How does purchase frequency vary by age?
- Do discounts have an impact on review ratings?
- Which payment methods are the most preferred?

## Key Business Insights

 1. **Purchase Patters by Demographics**
- Males who are >= 45 years old account for about 32% of orders (1260/3900 orders) and Females within the same age range account for about 15% of total orders. Together they make up about 47% of total orders
- This is reflected in the amount of revnue generated by this age group. Similar to purchases, the older males generate about 32% of the total revenue ($75,633/$233,081) and the older females generate about 15% of the total revenue ($35,224/$233,081).
- Older males and females place the most orders, therefore marketing efforts should target the older customers (>= 45 year olds) to drive up sales.

2. **Product and Category Analysis**
- Out of the 4 categories (Clothing, Accesories, Footwear and Outerwear), 45% of the orders and sales come from the clothing category (1,737/3,900 of orders and $104,264/$233,081 of sales)
- Accesories follow closely with 32% of both  orders and sales.
- The top 10 best selling items (Blouses, Jewelry, Pants, Shirts, Dresses, Sweaters, Jackets, Belts, Sunglasses and Coats) are all in the Clothing and Accesories except for Coats (Outerwear)
- Although Clothing and Accesories ar ethe best selling categories, there is a gap that can be filled with the coats in the outerwear category by increasing discounts and promotions to boosts sales in this category
- The Top 5 locations in terms of sales are Montana, Illinois, California, Idaho and Nevada respectively. However, the marginal difference in sales between the locations is very small ~0.02%

3. Impact of Discounts and Subscriptions
- *Full-price non-subscribers** drive ~57% of total revenue with the highest average order value ($60.13)
- **Subscribers** show the strongest repeat purchase signal (avg 26.08 previous purchases vs ~25.1 for others)
- Discounts are associated with **slightly lower** average order value (~$1–1.20 less per transaction)
- Customer satisfaction (review ratings) remains stable (~3.73–3.76) regardless of discount or subscription status  

**Main Recommendations**  
- Outerwear category has growth potential, focus marketing efforts on this category to drive up sales
- Prioritize growing the subscription base for better long-term retention; use discounts more tactically (acquisition, inventory clearance) rather than as a default lever.
